8  # DMRG (in development)
9  [Density matrix renormalization group](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Density_matrix_renormalization_group) (DMRG) is a variational numerical technique to study the low-energy physics of many-body quantum systems.
10 
11  This algorithm constructs and minimizes trial wave functions, in the shape of [Matrix Product States](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Matrix_product_state) (MPS), iteratively in order to find the ground state of one-dimensional quantum systems with high precision.
12 
13  This implementation loosely follows the steps outlined in:
14 
15  > [Phase Diagram of the Anisotropic Spin-2 XXZ Model: Infinite-System Density Matrix Renormalization Group Study.](https://arxiv.org/abs/1212.6255)<br>
16  > by Kjäll, Zaletel, Mong, Bardarson, and Pollmann. Physical Review B 87 (23): 235106. <br>
17 
18  > [Efficient Numerical Simulations Using Matrix-Product States](http://quantumtensor.pks.mpg.de/wp-content/uploads/2016/06/notes_1.pdf)<br>
19  > by Frank Pollmann. <br>
20 
21  > [The density-matrix renormalization group in the age of matrix product states](https://arxiv.org/abs/1008.3477)<br>
22  > by Ulrich Schollwöck. <br>
23

25  ## Notation
26 
27  The *Vidal canonical form*, i.e. \f$\Gamma\Lambda\Gamma\Lambda\f$"..., is used throughout this code.
28  In code we denote
29 
30  - \f$\Gamma \rightarrow\f$ `G`.
31  - \f$\Lambda \rightarrow\f$ `L`.
32 
33  ## Tensor index order convention.
34  The tensor index order used here follows the convention:
35  - physical indices first, from left to right or for MPO's, up to down.
36  - other dimensions (like bond dimensions) next, from left to right.
37 
38  #### Example:
39  Consider for some position \f$n\f$ on the chain \f$\Gamma = \Gamma^{\sigma_n}_{a,b}\f$.
40 Here \f$\sigma_n \in [-1,1]\f$ is a particle with local (physical) dimension \f$d\f$ = 2, and \f$a,b\f$ are the remaining dimensions, in practice they are
41 bond dimensions of \f$\Lambda^{n-1}\f$ and \f$\Lambda^{n}\f$, respectively, which can be numbers \f$\in [1,\chi]\f$.
42 
43 In diagrammatic tensor notation this is:
44 @verbatim
45  [d] 0
46  G = [a]__|__[b] = 1__|__2
47 @endverbatim
48 where after the second equality the index order is shown. In code this corresponds to
49 
50 \code{.cpp}
51  Textra::Tensor3 G(d,a,b);
52 \endcode
53 
54 Similarly, we have for \f$\Theta^{\sigma_n,\sigma_{n+1}}_{\chi_a,\chi_b}\f$:
55 
56 @verbatim
57  [d] [d] 0 1
58  Theta = [chia]___|___|___[chib] = 2 ___|___|___ 3
59 @endverbatim
60 
61 which in code reads
62 
63 \code{.cpp}
64  Textra::Tensor4 G(d,d,chia,chib);
65 \endcode
66
