12  # MPS Class
13  During the initial infinite-DMRG stage, sites A and B are swapped in each iteration to mimic the movement between even and odd positions
14  in a translationally invariant chain.
15 
16  ## Background
17  In both the infinite and finite DMRG algorithms, the MPS in this class will be used to construct \f$\Theta\f$, a two-site MPS tensor of rank 4:
18 
19  \f[
20  \Theta = \Lambda^B_{n-1} \Gamma^A_{n} \Lambda^A_{n} \Gamma^B_{n+1} \Lambda^B_{n+1}.
21  \f]
22 
23  > Note that the first \f$ \Lambda^B_{n-1} \f$ is special:
24  > - During infinite-DMRG \f$ \Lambda^B_{n-1} = \Lambda^B_{n+1} \f$ because we expect the MPS to be symmetric about \f$n\f$.
25  > - During finite-DMRG it is simply the bond directly to the left of \f$n\f$.
26 
27  ### Diagram
28 
29  In diagrammatic tensor notation the two-site MPS is:
30 @verbatim
31  [d] [d]
32  Theta = [chia]__.__|__.__|__.__[chib]
33 
34  LB GA LA GB LB
35 @endverbatim
36  where the values in [ ] denote the dimension of each leg of the full tensor:
37  - \f$d\f$ = local (or physical) dimension. Default is 2 for spin-1/2, Ising spins or qubits.
38  - \f$\chi_a\f$ = Left dimension equivalent to bond dimension of \f$\Lambda^B_{n-1}\f$
39  - \f$\chi_b\f$ = Right dimension equivalent to bond dimension of \f$\Lambda^B_{n+1}\f$
40 
41 
42  ### Tensor index order convention: See the <a href="index.html">Home page</a>.
43 */
